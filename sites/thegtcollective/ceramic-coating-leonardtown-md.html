Why correction comes before the coating

A ceramic coating is a semi-permanent glass-like layer bonded to your paint. It locks in everything beneath it — including swirl marks, water etching, and oxidation if they're still there. That's why some shops can quote less: they skip the correction and seal the defects in.

We don't. Every coating at The GT Collective begins with multi-stage machine polishing under controlled studio lighting. Only when the paint is genuinely defect-free does the coating go on. The result is depth and gloss your paint never had from the factory, protected for years.

You also get the full interior treatment — vacuum, shampoo, clean and protect — included with every coating package. Most shops charge that as an add-on. We consider it part of doing the whole job.

Ceramic coating questions, answered straight

How much does ceramic coating cost here?

Packages start at $1,200, and that number already includes full paint correction and interior detailing. Final pricing depends on vehicle size and paint condition, confirmed at consultation — we'll tell you exactly what your car needs and nothing it doesn't.

How long does it last?

Professional-grade coatings deliver multi-year protection with proper washing. Because we're local to Leonardtown, you can swing by for inspections and maintenance instead of mailing questions to a brand's support line.

Is it worth it for a daily driver?

Usually, yes. The biggest day-to-day benefit isn't the gloss — it's how much easier the vehicle is to keep clean. Bugs, bird droppings, and road film release with far less effort, and UV resistance keeps the paint from dulling.

Can you coat over scratches and swirls?

We won't. The coating would preserve them under glass, permanently. That's why correction comes first on every job — it's the difference between a coating that impresses for years and one you regret in a month.

Is the interior really included?

Yes — vacuum, shampoo, clean and protect, on every coating package. If deeper restoration is needed, we quote it openly at consultation before any work begins.
