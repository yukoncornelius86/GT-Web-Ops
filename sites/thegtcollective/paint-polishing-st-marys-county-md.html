What paint polishing does

Paint polishing is controlled machine work that refines the clear coat. It can reduce wash marring, swirl marks, haze, oxidation, and some water-spot etching so the paint reflects more cleanly.

It is not a wipe-on shine product, and it is not a promise to remove every scratch. The right process starts with inspection, paint condition, vehicle history, and a realistic conversation about what can improve safely.

Polishing vs correction

Paint polishing is often one step within a larger paint correction process. If your vehicle needs deeper multi-stage work, it may be quoted as paint correction. If the finish is being protected long-term, polishing is usually the right prep before ceramic coating.

Common questions

Can paint polishing remove all scratches?

No. Polishing can reduce many lighter defects, but deep scratches may remain if removal would thin the clear coat too aggressively.

Is polishing needed before ceramic coating?

Usually, yes. Ceramic coating locks in the finish underneath it. Polishing and correction help the paint look its best before protection is applied.
