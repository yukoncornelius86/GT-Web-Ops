Correction removes defects. It doesn't hide them.

Glazes and filler waxes can make swirl marks disappear — until the first rain. True paint correction is different: machine polishing carefully levels the clear coat itself, so the defects are gone, not masked.

Paint correction questions, answered straight

How long does paint correction take?

Depending on the vehicle's size and condition, correction is typically a full-day to multi-day process. We don't rush jobs and we don't book work we can't do to standard — your consultation will include an honest timeline.

How is this different from a regular detail?

A detail cleans the surface. Correction repairs it. Machine polishing physically levels the clear coat to remove swirls, scratches, etching, and oxidation — work that no wash, wax, or sealant can do.

Will the swirls come back?

Not on their own — correction removes them permanently. New marring comes from unsafe washing, so we send every client home with a simple safe-wash routine. A ceramic coating on top makes the finish far more resistant going forward.

Can every scratch be removed?

Honest answer: no. Scratches deep enough to catch a fingernail usually pass through the clear coat and need paint work, not polishing. We'll tell you which defects correction can eliminate and which it can only improve — before you commit to anything.

Is the interior included?

Yes — vacuum, shampoo, clean and protect come standard with every correction. If your interior needs deeper restoration, we quote it openly at consultation.
